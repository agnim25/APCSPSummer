An Abstract on Why We Lost Our Privacy

By Agnim Agarwal

We are especially conscious of our personal information landing into the wrong hands when we think about data theft or loss. Reports like one about the British tax office have become fairy common (Abelson, Ledeen, & Lewis, 2011). Why then, do we encounter problems with loss of privacy?

To Save Time (and Money)

Many times, people risk their privacy simply for convenience. If there is a shortcut available, most people will take it, even if it means spending a few bucks. An example of this circumstance is the Massachusetts Fast Lane's monthly bill. It shows where and when you got off the highway, to the nearest second. It also shows where you got off and how far you went. What does happen, however, is this information is used in divorce and child custody cases, resulting in extreme loss of privacy. Another example involving grocery stores is the use of loyalty cards. Loyalty cards track individual item purchases, which are not traced by credit cards. This information is exploited by companies as it can be spread to other organizations.

Convenience of the Customer

Many online shopping retailers, including Amazon, make use of "Convenience of the Customer". By tracking your searches and purchase history, companies can recommend other products, leading to further purchases.

In addition, several sites use cookies, small text files left on your computer that are available to you the next time you visit that website. One common example is the shopping cart.

Social Media

Many people enjoy sharing highlights of their life on social media sites, and it has its benefits. However, the release of basic personal information without privacy tools can be used for identity theft and other malicious activities. One example of this occuring is how burglars use Facebook to track people while on vacation. It is very natural to post details about your vacation while you're away, but this also encourages thievery as it lets them know that you're not home. For this reason, social media must be used with great caution.
